Overview

What happened, in three sentences

A family-owned plumbing company with 15 years of history was generating 70% of new business through Google Ads with monthly spend climbing to $6,800. Over 8 months we rebuilt the website around emergency service, fully optimized their Google Business Profile, and executed a local SEO campaign targeting 60+ high-intent keywords.

Emergency service calls grew from 22 to 94 per month — a 327% increase — while ad spend was cut by 65% as organic calls replaced paid leads. Monthly ad spend dropped from $6,800 to $2,400.

Before We Started

FAQ

Common questions

How long did this project take?

The full engagement ran 8 months, from the initial GBP claim and website rebuild through the final CRO optimization phase.

How long before emergency call volume increased?

First noticeable increases appeared around month 3 after the website rebuild and GBP optimization. The largest gains came between months 4 and 6 as the SEO campaign matured.

How did you reduce ad spend while growing leads?

Organic traffic and GBP visibility replaced paid channels as the primary lead source. As local rankings improved for emergency keywords, ad spend was scaled back from $6,800 to $2,400 per month without losing volume.

Which tactic moved fastest?

The GBP optimization and website rebuild produced the fastest results — emergency call routing and click-to-call CTAs started converting within weeks of launch.

What tools were used?

See the Tools & Platforms section above. Call tracking was integrated throughout the site to attribute every inbound call to a specific source.

How were results measured?

Every inbound call was tracked through the call tracking system and matched to its source (organic, GBP, paid). Revenue data came from the client's CRM, and ranking data from Search Console and Ahrefs.
